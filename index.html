Eva Boilley

Arbois, FRANCE - windcrafter@hotmail.com

Oil paintings for the environment
Are you eco-anxious ? Me too, and we have good reasons to be. This is why I ditched acrylics and solvents for mineral pigments and linseed oil and started painting landscapes of my hopes and fears for our civilisations by way of figurative magic realism.
In this era of environmental disasters and climate crisis, I depict the victory of the plant kingdom prevailing over human follies. In a future like any other, where mankind hasn’t dragged everything in its fall, I imagine with humour and horror how a magnificent hyperflora begins the planet’s lengthy healing process. The land may have dried out, we might all have perished, but plants know more about water, survival and interconnectedness than we do.
I’m inspired by mountain landscapes, beautiful forests, my mother’s garden and manmade buildings and objects which are or will be discarded, abandoned or destroyed. I invite you to find quiet beauty and hope in iridescent post-apocalyptic desolation. I also invite you to take arms against climate change and not let my paintings come true.
